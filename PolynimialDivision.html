Polynomial Division

Master Long Division and Synthetic Division with Interactive Examples

Introduction to Polynomial Division

Polynomial division is a method for dividing a polynomial by another polynomial of equal or lower degree. The two main methods are:

  • Long Division: A method similar to numerical long division that works for any polynomial division
  • Synthetic Division: A shortcut method that works only when dividing by a linear polynomial (x - c)

In both methods, we express the result as:

Dividend = Divisor × Quotient + Remainder

This presentation will guide you through both methods with interactive examples, practice problems, and a quiz to test your understanding.

Key Terminology

  • Dividend: The polynomial being divided
  • Divisor: The polynomial we're dividing by
  • Quotient: The result of the division
  • Remainder: What's left after division

Long Division of Polynomials

Long division of polynomials follows a process similar to numerical long division. Let's work through examples of increasing difficulty.

Example 1: (x² + 5x + 6) ÷ (x + 2)

Long Division Steps

  1. Arrange both polynomials in descending order of exponents
  2. Divide the first term of the dividend by the first term of the divisor
  3. Multiply the entire divisor by this result and subtract from the dividend
  4. Bring down the next term and repeat until all terms have been processed
  5. The final result is the quotient plus any remainder over the divisor

Synthetic Division

Synthetic division is a shortcut method that works when dividing by a linear polynomial of the form (x - c). It's more efficient than long division for these cases.

Example 1: (x³ - 2x² - 5x + 6) ÷ (x - 3)

Synthetic Division Steps

  1. Write the root (c) of the divisor (x - c) to the left
  2. Write the coefficients of the dividend to the right
  3. Bring down the first coefficient
  4. Multiply by the root and add to the next coefficient
  5. Repeat until all coefficients are processed
  6. The last number is the remainder, the others are coefficients of the quotient

Comparing Long Division and Synthetic Division

Aspect Long Division Synthetic Division
Applicability Works for any polynomial division Only works when dividing by (x - c)
Complexity More steps, more writing Fewer steps, less writing
Visual clarity Shows the complete process clearly More compact but less intuitive
Missing terms Must include 0 coefficients for missing terms Must include 0 coefficients for missing terms
Best for General polynomial division, teaching concepts Quick division by linear factors, polynomial evaluation

When to Use Each Method

Use Long Division when:

  • The divisor is not linear (degree > 1)
  • You're learning polynomial division for the first time
  • You need to clearly show each step of the process

Use Synthetic Division when:

  • The divisor is linear (x - c)
  • You need a quick calculation
  • You're evaluating a polynomial at x = c
