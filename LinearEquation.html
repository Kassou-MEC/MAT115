What's a Linear Equation Anyway?

Think of a linear equation as a perfectly balanced seesaw! ‚öñÔ∏è It's an equation where the variable (usually x) has a power of 1, and both sides must be equal. No squares, no cubes, just straight-line simplicity!

Examples: x + 5 = 12, 3x - 7 = 14, 2(x + 3) = 16

The Simplest Equations in Town!

One-step equations are like opening a simple door - you just need one key! üóùÔ∏è These require only ONE operation to isolate the variable.

Multi-Step Equations: Level Up! üéÆ

When One Step Isn't Enough!

Multi-step equations are like complex puzzles that need several moves to solve! üß© They involve multiple operations and sometimes parentheses, fractions, or variables on both sides.

Strategy: Work backwards through the order of operations!

The Multi-Step Master Solver! üîß

3x + 5 = 17

The Order of Operations (PEMDAS) Reversed! üîÑ

When solving, we undo operations in reverse order:

  1. Parentheses ‚Üí Handle these last when solving!
  2. Exponents ‚Üí Rarely in linear equations
  3. Multiplication/Division ‚Üí Undo these second
  4. Addition/Subtraction ‚Üí Undo these first
EASY

Basic Two-Step

Problem: 2x + 6 = 14

Step 1: 2x + 6 - 6 = 14 - 6

Step 2: 2x = 8

Step 3: x = 4

Subtract first, then divide!
MEDIUM

Variables on Both Sides

Problem: 5x + 3 = 2x + 12

Step 1: 5x - 2x + 3 = 2x - 2x + 12

Step 2: 3x + 3 = 12

Step 3: 3x = 9

Step 4: x = 3

Get all x's on one side first!
HARD

Distributive Property

Problem: 3(x - 4) = 21

Step 1: 3x - 12 = 21

Step 2: 3x - 12 + 12 = 21 + 12

Step 3: 3x = 33

Step 4: x = 11

Distribute first, then solve!
EXPERT

Fraction Madness!

Problem: (2x + 1)/3 = 5

Step 1: 2x + 1 = 15

Step 2: 2x = 14

Step 3: x = 7

Multiply by denominator to clear fractions!

The Multi-Step Game Plan! üéØ

  1. Simplify both sides (distribute, combine like terms)
  2. Move variables to one side
  3. Move constants to the other side
  4. Divide or multiply to isolate the variable
  5. Check your answer!

Symbols: > (greater than), < (less than), ‚â• (greater than or equal to), ‚â§ (less than or equal to)

The Great Flip Rule! üîÑ

REMEMBER: When you multiply or divide by a negative number, flip the inequality sign!

Absolute Value: Distance Makes the Heart Grow Fonder! |üíù|

What's All This About Distance?

Absolute value measures the distance from zero on a number line! üìè It's always positive (or zero) because distance can't be negative!

Key Insight: |x| = 5 means x is 5 units away from zero, so x = 5 or x = -5!

The Two-Solution Mystery! üïµÔ∏è

Most absolute value equations have TWO solutions!

If |x - 3| = 5, then:

x - 3 = 5 OR x - 3 = -5

x = 8 OR x = -2

Absolute Value Equation Solver! üéØ

EASY

Basic Absolute Value

Problem: |x| = 7

Solution 1: x = 7

Solution 2: x = -7

Both 7 and -7 are 7 units from zero!
MEDIUM

Absolute Value with Addition

Problem: |x + 3| = 5

Case 1: x + 3 = 5 ‚Üí x = 2

Case 2: x + 3 = -5 ‚Üí x = -8

Two cases, two solutions!
HARD

Absolute Value with Coefficients

Problem: |3x - 9| = 12

Case 1: 3x - 9 = 12 ‚Üí x = 7

Case 2: 3x - 9 = -12 ‚Üí x = -1

Always check both solutions!
EXPERT

Absolute Value Inequalities

Problem: |x - 2| < 5

Meaning: -5 < x - 2 < 5

Solution: -3 < x < 7

Distance from 2 is less than 5!

Absolute Value Survival Guide! üÜò

  • |expression| = positive number: Two solutions!
  • |expression| = 0: One solution!
  • |expression| = negative number: No solution!
  • |expression| < number: Compound inequality!
  • |expression| > number: Union of two inequalities!
